What is Carbon Footprint? Carbon Footprint is a term that is used to refer to the carbon or CO2 emitted in the atmosphere due to various activities.

Why is it important to off-set it?

The climate crisis is now considered our gravest existential threat. 50% of climate changing pollutants have been pumped into our atmosphere—from power stations, cars, agriculture—since just 1990, and this amount is growing every second. The types of carbon offset projects that are implemented are diverse. They range from forestry sequestration projects (which remove CO2 from the atmosphere when trees grow) to energy efficiency and renewable energy projects (which reduce future CO2 emissions in the atmosphere).
